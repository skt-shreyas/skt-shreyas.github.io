About Me

I am a Research Associate at the IACV Lab, IISc Bangalore, where my research focuses on multimodal learning, robustness, and interpretable AI systems. My work lies at the intersection of computer vision and language models, with an emphasis on designing reliable, resource-efficient, and reasoning-driven frameworks for real-world challenges.

My research and development efforts broadly explore:

  • Multimodal learning using Vision-Language Models, with applications in analysing complex real-world information
  • Resource-efficient and data-efficient AI systems designed for low-resource and limited-annotation settings
  • Robust, uncertainty-aware, and reasoning-driven machine learning frameworks

Previously, I worked as an AI Engineer at Grainscope AI, where I developed computer vision pipelines for industrial grain quality assessment. My technical contributions to the Grain Analyser product were formally recognized through a government-issued copyright certificate obtained by the company. I also held research roles at CAIR-DRDO Bangalore. I hold a Bachelor of Technology from BIT Sindri, Dhanbad Jharkhand, graduating with a CGPA of 8.4/10. My undergraduate thesis on AI-driven airfoil optimisation was awarded 2nd Best Thesis in the department.

Beyond research and engineering, I have a strong inclination toward visual creativity and design. I enjoy painting, exploring digital aesthetics, and blending artistic thinking with technical problem-solving. I consider myself a workaholic who genuinely enjoys deep, focused work, while also embracing a minimalist lifestyle centered on clarity, simplicity, and intentional living. I am equally passionate about mentoring, leadership, and community-driven initiatives.
